## Design Context

### Users
Home cooks of all skill levels, browsing on phones and desktops. Two modes: quick recipe lookup (task-focused, needs fast scanning) and leisurely browsing (discovery, inspiration). Bulgarian audience.

### Brand Personality
Warm, honest, generous. Like a friend who cooks well and shares freely — no pretension, no food-blog theatrics. Everyday recipes, everyday people.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Both light and dark mode
- **Light**: Warm cream and parchment tones, soft earthy accents — feels like a well-used cookbook
- **Dark**: Deep warm neutrals (not cold charcoal), candlelit feeling — not a tech dashboard
- **References**: The feeling of a handwritten recipe card, a Sunday kitchen, a market in Sofia
- **Anti-references**: Sterile food-magazine minimalism, neon/gradient "foodie app" aesthetics, generic card-grid recipe sites
- **No specific colors to avoid**

### Design Principles
1. **Scannability first** — home cooks need to find cook time, servings, difficulty at a glance
2. **Warmth over polish** — textures, hand-feel, imperfect details beat clinical precision
3. **Generous whitespace** — breathing room makes food feel appetizing, not cramped
4. **Typography carries mood** — font choices should feel as curated as the recipes
5. **Motion with purpose** — subtle, never showy; supports the eye, doesn't perform for it
